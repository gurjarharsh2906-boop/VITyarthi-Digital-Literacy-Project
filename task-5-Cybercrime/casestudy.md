# Task 5: Cybercrime Case Study - UPI/Online Payment Fraud

### 1. Detailed Description of the Crime
Unified Payments Interface (UPI) fraud is a sophisticated form of financial cybercrime that leverages social engineering to exploit the "trust factor" in digital transactions. Unlike traditional hacking, this crime does not require breaching a bank’s security system; instead, it manipulates the human user into voluntarily authorizing a theft. In the context of a college campus like VIT Bhopal, where students use UPI for everything from mess payments to stationary, this is a high-risk threat.

### 2. Step-by-Step Scenario (The "Refund" Scam)
* **The Initial Contact:** A student receives a high-pressure phone call from an individual claiming to be a "Technical Support Executive" from a popular payment app or a delivery service.
* **The False Claim:** The attacker claims that an "accidental transaction" of ₹2,000 was made to the student's account and must be reversed immediately to avoid account freezing.
* **The "Collect" Request:** Instead of providing a bank account number, the attacker sends a "Collect Request" notification to the student’s UPI app.
* **The Fatal Error:** The attacker instructs the student to "Open the app and enter your PIN to verify your identity." The student, under stress, enters their UPI PIN.
* **The Consequence:** Because a UPI PIN is only required to send money, the student has unknowingly authorized a transfer to the attacker. The funds are instantly debited.

### 3. Target Audience & Victim Profile
The primary targets are "Digital Natives" (college students) who are comfortable with technology but may be overconfident in their security habits. Attackers target those who are likely to be distracted or in a rush, such as students during exam weeks or registration periods.

### 4. Impact and Consequences
* **Financial Loss:** Immediate loss of personal savings or monthly allowance.
* **Psychological Impact:** A significant loss of confidence in digital tools, leading to "digital anxiety".
* **Security Risk:** Once a victim falls for one scam, their contact information is often sold on the dark web to other scammers as a "soft target".
