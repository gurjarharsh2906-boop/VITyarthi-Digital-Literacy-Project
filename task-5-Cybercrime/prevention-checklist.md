#  Stay Safe Online: A Guide for VIT Bhopal Students

As a Student Digital Ambassador, I have compiled this checklist to help you recognize and avoid common online threats. Following these 8 actionable steps will significantly improve your digital security.

###  UPI & Financial Safety
1. **The "PIN for Pay" Rule:** Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. A PIN is only required to send money or check your account balance. If someone asks you to enter a PIN to get a "refund" or "prize," it is a scam.
2. **Verify the Requestor:** Before clicking on any UPI "Collect Request" notification, verify the identity of the person or business through a separate, trusted communication channel.

###  Account & Password Security
3. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Activate 2FA on all critical accounts, including your college email, GitHub, and LinkedIn, to add an extra layer of protection beyond just a password.
4. **Use Strong, Unique Passwords:** Avoid using easily guessable information like your registration number or birthdate. Use a combination of uppercase letters, numbers, and special symbols.

###  Safe Browsing & Communication
5. **Inspect Links Before Clicking:** Always hover your mouse over a link in an email to see the actual destination URL. If the address looks suspicious or doesn't match the sender, do not click it.
6. **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks:** Never log into your bank account or enter credit card details while connected to unsecured public Wi-Fi networks in cafes or transit hubs.
7. **Official App Sources Only:** Download banking, social media, and academic apps only from the official Google Play Store or Apple App Store to avoid "malware-laden" cloned apps.

###  How to Report Cybercrime
8. **Use Official Reporting Channels:** If you or someone you know falls victim to a digital scam, immediate reporting is crucial.
    * **National Cyber Crime Portal:** cybercrime.gov.in
    * **24/7 National Helpline:** Dial 1930
